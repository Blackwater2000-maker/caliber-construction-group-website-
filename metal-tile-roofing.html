Stone-Coated Metal Tile

Why Stone-Coated Metal Tile Makes Sense for Florida

Tile Look. Steel Strength.

The Best of Both Worlds

Stone-coated steel roofing delivers the aesthetic of clay barrel tile with the structural performance of steel. Panels are roll-formed into the classic S-profile barrel shape, then coated with acrylic and embedded with stone chip granules — creating a dark charcoal surface that is visually indistinguishable from genuine clay tile at a fraction of the weight and cost.

For Florida homeowners who want the Mediterranean aesthetic without the structural loading requirements of true clay tile, stone-coated metal tile is the ideal solution. These systems are Florida hurricane-rated, carry 50-year warranties, and are eligible for the FORTIFIED Roof Program — which can qualify homeowners for significant insurance premium reductions.

We install DECRA, Gerard, Boral, and equivalent system brands. Every installation is permitted to Florida Building Code and managed by a dedicated on-site senior team member.

  • Barrel S-profile — matches clay tile aesthetic
  • Dark charcoal with stone chip granule coating
  • Florida hurricane wind-rated
  • 50-year warranty
  • FORTIFIED-eligible — insurance discount potential
  • Lighter than clay — no structural upgrade needed
  • Permits pulled and managed
