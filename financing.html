Understanding HELOCs

Why Pull Equity Instead of Paying Cash?

Most Central Florida homeowners have seen significant equity gains over the past several years. A HELOC lets you tap into that equity at typically lower interest rates than personal loans or credit cards — without selling investments, cashing out a 401(k), or depleting an emergency fund.

Think of it this way: your home is already worth more than you paid for it. A HELOC converts that paper gain into a working line of credit — funds you draw only as needed, paying interest only on what you've actually borrowed. Your roof gets done now. Your investment portfolio stays intact.

HELOCs use your home as collateral. Interest may be tax-deductible when funds are used for home improvement. Consult a financial advisor for guidance specific to your situation. All loans subject to credit approval.

1

Check Your Available Equity

Your lender determines your HELOC limit based on your home's current value minus your remaining mortgage balance — often 80–85% combined loan-to-value.

2

Apply Online in Minutes

Both Fusion and appli offer fully digital applications. No in-person appraisal required for certain HELOC products. Approvals can come quickly.

3

Draw Only What You Need

Unlike a personal loan lump sum, a HELOC is a revolving line. Draw for the roof now — and the remaining credit stays available for future projects.

4

Pay Interest Only on Draws

During the draw period, you typically pay interest only on what you've borrowed — keeping monthly obligations manageable while the work is completed.

Financing Questions, Answered

How do I get started with financing through one of your recommended lenders?
You can apply directly on the lender's website using the links above, or call Caliber at (407) 517-8200 and we can connect you with the right contact at each lender based on your situation.
Do I need to be approved for financing before Caliber starts work?
We recommend having financing in place or approved before we schedule installation — this ensures no delays once your project begins. We're happy to work alongside your lender's timeline.
What's the difference between a HELOC and a home equity loan?
A HELOC is a revolving line of credit — you draw what you need and pay interest only on what you borrow, similar to a credit card secured by your home. A home equity loan is a lump sum at a fixed rate. Fusion Home Loans offers both options and can help you determine which fits your situation.
What if my insurance is covering part of the replacement cost?
Financing can cover your deductible, any scope beyond what insurance covers, or your full out-of-pocket if insurance is in dispute. Talk to us — we'll help you understand your total exposure before you apply for financing.
Does applying for financing affect my credit score?
A full loan application typically involves a hard credit inquiry. Some lenders offer pre-qualification with a soft pull that doesn't affect your score. Ask your lender about pre-qualification options before submitting a full application.
