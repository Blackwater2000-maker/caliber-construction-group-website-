CertainTeed Landmark® Shingles: Florida Homeowner's Complete Guide

Caliber Construction Group · June 2026 · 6 min read

When Florida homeowners are weighing their shingle options, CertainTeed Landmark® consistently earns a place at the top of the list. CertainTeed is one of North America's oldest and most trusted roofing manufacturers — with over 100 years of history producing building products — and Landmark® is their flagship architectural shingle. It's a product with serious credentials, engineered to perform in the demanding conditions Florida homeowners face every year.

At Caliber Construction Group, we install CertainTeed products across Central Florida and know this shingle's performance profile thoroughly. Here's what every Florida homeowner should know before choosing it for their home.

What Are CertainTeed Landmark® Shingles?

Landmark® shingles are dual-layered laminated architectural shingles — meaning two layers of material are bonded together to create a single, heavy-duty shingle with a dimensional, wood-shake-like appearance. The foundation is a fiberglass-reinforced mat, which provides dimensional stability and resistance to tearing under high-wind stress.

CertainTeed offers Landmark® in a wide range of colors across multiple product lines, including Landmark® Solaris (solar-reflective), Landmark® PRO, and Landmark® Premium. The color range is one of the broadest available from any manufacturer, giving homeowners significant flexibility to match existing exterior finishes, HOA requirements, or personal design preferences.

NailTrak® Technology

One of the defining features of CertainTeed Landmark® is NailTrak® technology — an engineered nailing zone built directly into the shingle surface. NailTrak® provides an extra-wide, clearly marked nailing area that makes accurate fastening easier and more consistent for installers, particularly on steep-slope roofs where precise nailing is more challenging.

Why does this matter? In roofing, the quality of the nail placement is one of the most important factors in wind resistance. Nails placed outside the correct zone — too high or too low — significantly reduce the shingle's wind-holding strength. NailTrak® is designed to minimize installer error and ensure every shingle is fastened as the manufacturer intends, which directly supports the wind warranty coverage and real-world performance in Florida storm conditions.

StreakFighter® Algae Protection

Florida's combination of heat, humidity, and rainfall creates near-ideal conditions for algae growth on roof surfaces. Those dark, streaky stains that appear on shingles over time are caused by Gloeocapsa magma — a blue-green algae that feeds on the limestone filler in standard asphalt shingles.

CertainTeed addresses this with StreakFighter® algae protection, available on Landmark® shingles. StreakFighter® embeds algae-resistant granules into the shingle surface that inhibit algae growth and keep the roof looking clean over time. CertainTeed backs this with a 25-year algae resistance warranty — a meaningful commitment for a Florida roof that will see significant moisture and biological stress year after year.

For homeowners in Central Florida where algae staining on roofs is extremely common, choosing a shingle with long-term algae protection isn't just cosmetic — it's a practical investment in maintaining curb appeal and property value.

Why It's a Strong Choice for Florida

Several characteristics make CertainTeed Landmark® particularly well-suited to Florida's climate and homeowner needs:

  • Class 3 Impact Resistance: UL 2218 Class 3 impact resistance is one of the highest available for standard architectural shingles. In Florida, this rating can qualify homeowners for insurance premium discounts — a meaningful financial benefit given how high Florida homeowners insurance has become. Check with your insurer to confirm eligibility.
  • StreakFighter® for Humidity: The 25-year algae resistance warranty is specifically engineered for humid climates. Florida's weather profile is one of the most challenging for algae growth in the country, and long-term protection matters here more than in northern markets.
  • Wide Color Palette: CertainTeed's color range is extensive, giving Florida homeowners flexibility to complement stucco, brick, siding, and other architectural elements common in Central Florida residential construction.
  • Dual-Layer Construction: The laminated design provides greater structural integrity and a more premium appearance than standard single-layer shingles — important for homeowners who want their roof to reflect the quality of their property.

The Limited Lifetime Warranty

CertainTeed backs Landmark® shingles with a Limited Lifetime Warranty for the life of the structure when installed on owner-occupied residential properties. Key warranty components include:

  • SureStart™ Protection: The first 10 years of the warranty include SureStart™ coverage — a non-prorated period during which CertainTeed covers 100% of eligible material and labor costs. This is the most valuable phase of any shingle warranty.
  • Algae Resistance Warranty: StreakFighter® carries a dedicated 25-year algae resistance warranty — separate from and in addition to the product warranty.
  • Transferability: The warranty can be transferred to a subsequent homeowner (with reduced coverage terms), which can be a selling point when listing the property.

When installed by a CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster™ or Master ShingleRocker® certified contractor as part of the Integrity Roof System®, homeowners may qualify for enhanced system warranties with extended labor and material coverage.
