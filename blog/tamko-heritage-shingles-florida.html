TAMKO Heritage® Shingles: A Florida Roofing Guide

Caliber Construction Group · June 2026 · 5 min read

In a market dominated by large public companies, TAMKO Building Products stands apart as a family-owned American manufacturer that has been producing roofing materials since 1944. That independence and long operating history matter — TAMKO's quality control decisions are made by the family that owns the company, not by quarterly earnings targets. Heritage® is their flagship architectural shingle line and one of the most established laminated shingle products in the Southeast.

For Florida homeowners evaluating shingle options, TAMKO Heritage® offers a strong combination of wind performance, warranty coverage, and the reliability that comes from nearly 80 years of domestic production. Here's what you need to know.

What Are TAMKO Heritage® Shingles?

Heritage® shingles are laminated architectural shingles — a dual-layer design that creates a dimensional profile with deep shadow lines that replicate the textured appearance of natural wood shake or slate. The laminated construction delivers greater thickness, visual depth, and durability compared to standard three-tab shingles.

TAMKO offers Heritage® in a wide color range spanning neutrals, earth tones, and cooler charcoals, giving homeowners the flexibility to coordinate with stucco, siding, brick, and other Florida exterior finishes. The dimensional shadow effect created by the layered profile elevates curb appeal significantly compared to flat, single-layer alternatives.

Wind Performance

Wind resistance is the defining performance metric for Florida homeowners, and Heritage® delivers meaningfully here. When installed according to TAMKO's High Wind Application Instructions with the appropriate fastening pattern, Heritage® shingles carry a wind warranty of up to 130 MPH.

This is a significant figure. Most of Florida's residential construction will face sustained winds in the 90–120 MPH range during strong tropical storms and Category 1–2 hurricanes. A 130 MPH wind-warranted shingle, properly installed, provides documented margin above the majority of storm events a Central Florida home will experience over its lifetime.

The key phrase is "properly installed." Achieving the 130 MPH warranty requires following TAMKO's high wind fastening pattern — typically six nails per shingle rather than four, placed within the designated nail zone. This is exactly the installation standard Caliber follows on every Heritage® installation: manufacturer specification compliance is non-negotiable on a Florida roof.

The standard wind warranty without high wind application is 60 MPH — homeowners should confirm with their contractor that the high wind installation method is being used if 130 MPH coverage is the goal.

TAMKO Complete Roofing System

Like other leading manufacturers, TAMKO offers enhanced warranty coverage when their products are installed as a Complete Roofing System — meaning Heritage® shingles are installed alongside TAMKO-approved underlayment, starter strips, hip and ridge cap, and ventilation components.

When a Complete Roofing System is installed by a TAMKO Pro Certified Contractor, homeowners may qualify for Enhanced Limited System Warranties that extend material and labor coverage beyond the standard product warranty terms. This system warranty approach aligns incentives between the manufacturer, contractor, and homeowner — TAMKO stands behind the performance of the entire roof assembly, not just individual components in isolation.

For homeowners who want maximum warranty protection, specifying a complete TAMKO system installation through a certified contractor is the path to the broadest coverage available.

Why TAMKO Heritage® for Florida

Several factors make Heritage® a sound choice for Florida residential roofing:

  • 130 MPH Wind Warranty with Proper Installation: This is one of the strongest standard wind warranties available for a laminated architectural shingle, and it covers the realistic range of storm events a Central Florida home is likely to experience. Achieving this warranty requires correct installation — always confirm your contractor will follow the high wind application method.
  • Family-Owned Quality Control: TAMKO's ownership structure means decisions about product quality, manufacturing standards, and material sourcing are made by the founding family — not driven by public market pressures. For homeowners who value that kind of commitment, it matters.
  • Operating Since 1944: Nearly 80 years of continuous shingle production in the United States. That track record provides confidence that warranty claims will be honored and the company will remain a going concern for the life of the roof — a meaningful consideration when evaluating a product with a limited lifetime warranty.
  • Wide Color Range: Heritage® comes in a broad palette of colors suited to Florida's diverse residential architecture, from traditional earth-tone stucco homes to more contemporary builds with lighter exteriors.
